## What App Store Connect will also ask for

**App Information tab** (global, not per-language)
- **Primary category**: Lifestyle
- **Secondary category**: Reference *(optional, helps discovery)*
- **Content Rights**: "Does your app contain, show, or access third-party content?" → **No** (all hadiths are public-domain classical texts)
- **Age Rating**: run the questionnaire, answer No to everything → expect **4+**

**App Privacy tab** — the answer for this app is essentially "we collect nothing":
- **Location**: collected, linked to user, used for **App Functionality only**. Not tracked.
- **User Content** (Qaza logs, settings): **Not Collected** because it's SwiftData local only.
- **Identifiers / Diagnostics / Usage Data**: **Not Collected**.
- Tracking: **No**.

**Support URL** + **Privacy Policy URL**: both required. The privacy policy can literally be a one-pager — "Salah Clarity does not collect, store, or transmit personal data. Your location is used only to calculate prayer times and never leaves your device." Host it on GitHub Pages, Notion public page, or your own site.

**App Review Information**
- **Sign-in required?**: No.
- **Demo account**: leave empty.
- **Notes to reviewer** (optional but recommended, paste as-is):
```
Salah Clarity is an offline Islamic prayer-times app. The app requests
location permission on launch to calculate five-daily prayer times for
the user's coordinates. No data is transmitted to any server. All
content (hadiths, azkar, prayer-time algorithm) runs locally.

To test notifications, tap Settings → Send test notification. It
fires a notification in ~5 seconds with the bundled azan sound.
```
